renCal is a modern, open-source calendar app built for Omarchy and Linux.

It is designed for people who want a fast desktop calendar that feels native to their system, works well from the keyboard, and keeps calendar data in simple local files.

## What makes renCal different?

- **Plain files** — events are stored as `.ics` files on your machine.
- **Local-first** — your calendar remains available offline.
- **Provider sync** — connect calendars from Google, iCloud, Outlook, and CalDAV providers.
- **Natural input** — create events with phrases like “lunch tomorrow at 1pm”.
- **Keyboard-first** — navigate quickly with Vim-style shortcuts.
- **Omarchy native** — themes can follow your Omarchy system colors.

## How it works

renCal is powered by [Caldir](https://caldir.org), which represents calendars as directories of standard `.ics` files.

That means your events are not locked inside an app database. They can be synced, backed up, inspected, scripted, or edited with other tools that understand calendar files.

## Who is it for?

renCal is best for people who want:

- a clean desktop calendar for Linux,
- local access to their calendar data,
- sync with existing calendar accounts,
- keyboard-friendly workflows,
- and an app that fits naturally into Omarchy.
